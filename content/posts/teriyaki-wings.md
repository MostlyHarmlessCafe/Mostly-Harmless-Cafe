This is more about the sauce than the wings. This recipe is enough for around 10-12 wings and can make anything from a wet and saucy to sticky and candy-like, depending on how long you cook it.

# Ingredients
- &frac12; cup of soy sauce
- &frac14; cup of brown sugar
- 1 tbsp of gochujang
- 2 tbsp of honey
- 4 cloves of garlic (minced)
- 1 tbsp of ginger paste
- 1 tbsp of sesame oil
- Rice vinegar[^rice vinegar]
- 1 tsp of MSG[^MSG]
- slurry:
    - 2 tbsp of cornstarch
    - &frac14; cup of water
- Sesame seeds
- Green onions (thinly sliced)

# Instructions
1. To a wok on medium-low heat, add everything less the slurry components, sesame seeds, and green onion.
1. Let simmer for 3 minutes.
1. Do a final test for taste. Adjust with honey and rice vinegar depending on what you want. Add honey to make it sweeter. Add vinegar if it tastes a bit salty or bitter. Whatever it is now, it will be moreso when you serve it.
1. Add the slurry in increments until you get the thickness that you want.
1. Increase heat to medium and add the wings.
1. Toss the wings until they're evenly coated. The longer that you toss the wings, the more the sauce is going to cook onto them. This makes it less wet and more sticky. It progressively starts turning into a candy-like coating on the wings the longer that you cook it. If it's too sticky for you, you can add some water to make it a bit thinner and looser.
1. Place the wings on a plate or tray and garnish with sesame seeds and green onions.

[^rice vinegar]: I've never used the same amount of rice vinegar twice so you're going to add this to taste. You're going to add it until the sauce stops tasting overwhelmingly salty.
[^MSG]: This is to add "umami." If you don't add it, it's not going to ruin the dish, it just helps bring out the savoriness.
